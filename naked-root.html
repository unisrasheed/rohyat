Your New Plant Care Routine

You stick your finger in the soil to check the moisture, then you give it water according to the instructions. You do everything you're supposed to, but somehow your plant always seem to mysteriously die anyway. Sound familiar?

What’s not so obvious (and difficult to know with just a finger test or moisture meter) is the water that’s still sitting at the bottom of the planter. And when you add more water to these already wet roots, you make matters worse until finally, your plant falls victim to root rot.

The Cure

More Root Zone Oxygen

When roots stay wet, oxygen is depleted creating the perfect conditions for anaerobic disease, otherwise known as root rot.

The Naked Root planter breathes like a human lung, constantly delivering life sustaining oxygen to the entire root system.

With constant 360-degree root aeration and the perfect ratio of oxygen, water and soil, root rot is now a thing of the past.

Plants Thrive

Roots in a conventional planter have nowhere to go, they twist and turn and get root bound. If you don’t repot your plant frequently, the roots will eventually strangle your plant, causing poor growth and a steady decline.

Roots in a Naked Root planter won’t get root bound, they get “air root pruned”. When root tips are exposed to oxygen they stop growing outward, creating an explosion of productive inner root hairs.

With over 200% more root surface area, they’re able to absorb more water, oxygen and nutrients so plants can grow bigger and healthier with much less repotting.

Plants Thrive. You Thrive

Plants clean the air you breathe by consuming airborne pollutants through their leaves.

It’s been proven: the microbial life surrounding exposed roots consume even more pollutants at night, while you sleep. With a Naked Root, you get more than twice the air purifying power.

Plus, plants provide oxygen to help you breathe better, put you in a better mood, reduce stress, increase your concentration, and make you more creative.
